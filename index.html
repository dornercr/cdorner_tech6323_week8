Ethics in Educational Technology

Comprehensive guidelines for instructional designers fostering ethical technology integration in education

1

Equitable Access

Ensure all students have equal access to technology and digital resources, bridging the digital divide to support inclusive education.

ISTE
2

Student Privacy

Adhere to privacy laws like FERPA to safeguard student data and maintain confidentiality in all digital interactions.

U.S. Dept. of Education
3

Intellectual Property

Comply with copyright laws by using and sharing content legally, providing proper attribution, and teaching students about intellectual property rights.

U.S. Copyright Office
4

AI Ethics

Use AI responsibly by ensuring transparency, avoiding bias, and being accountable for AI-driven decisions in educational contexts.

UNESCO
5

Social Media Conduct

Maintain professionalism on social media by respecting boundaries and engaging appropriately with students and colleagues.

AASL
6

Accessibility

Implement Universal Design for Learning principles to create accessible instructional materials for all learners.

CAST
7

Student Data Ethics

Use student data ethically to inform instruction while preventing misuse that could harm student interests.

Data Quality Campaign
8

Continuous Reflection

Engage in ongoing learning and reflection on ethical practices related to emerging technologies in education.

NEA
